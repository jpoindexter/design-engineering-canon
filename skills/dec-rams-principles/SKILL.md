# Dieter Rams — 10 Principles of Good Design

A rubric for judging whether a design is *good*, not just functional. Good design is:

1. **Innovative** — advances with technology, never an end in itself.
2. **Useful** — emphasizes usefulness; discards anything that detracts from it.
3. **Aesthetic** — beauty is integral to usefulness; well-made objects affect wellbeing.
4. **Understandable** — clarifies structure; ideally self-explanatory.
5. **Unobtrusive** — neutral and restrained, leaving room for the user.
6. **Honest** — doesn't promise more than it delivers or manipulate.
7. **Long-lasting** — avoids fashion; stays relevant across years.
8. **Thorough to the last detail** — nothing arbitrary; care expresses respect for the user.
9. **Environmentally friendly** — conserves resources, minimizes waste.
10. **As little design as possible** — less, but better. Concentrate on the essential.

## How to apply

- Score a design candidate against all ten; weak scores name the work to do.
- #10 is the tiebreaker: when in doubt, remove. Decoration must justify itself against restraint.
- #6 (honest) maps directly to anti-dark-pattern work — no manipulation, no false promises.
